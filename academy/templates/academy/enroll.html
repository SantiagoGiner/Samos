Physics

You might not realize it, but physics and its achievements are all around you. For me, physics is one of the greatest achievements of humanity, which allows us to explain everything we see around us, from the behavior of subnuclear particles to the large-scale structure of stars and galaxy superclusters. Not only that, physics is behind all the major technological advancements of our era, including computers, wireless communication, batteries, transportation, and many others. I wish not only to teach you how to solve problems in physics and think analytically and critically, but I also want to teach you about the importance of physics, how it applies to our everyday life, and all the beauty it represents.

Mathematics

Mathematics is the basis of most everything that is done in modern science. It is the backbone of our understanding of the universe and it offers us a beautifully elegant framework upon which we can model real-world phenomena and make accurate predictions about them. Math is a language in it of itself, and speaking that language is one of the most useful skills one can learn, for it literally opens up a world of opportunites in which to work on and areas to explore. I hope to teach you not only mathematical concepts and abilities but also their real-world applications and importance.

Standardized Tests

I know exactly what you are going through--standardized tests for college applications are extremely stressful. I went through exactly the same issues as you might very likely be facing: not knowing where to start with all the material available for the tests, feeling anxious that you might not be fully prepared for them, thinking that this one test might decide the course of your studies. Well. First, let me tell you that you need not worry that much: standardized tests like the SAT, ACT, SAT Subject Tests, AP, etc., are indeed an important factor in your application but not in the least the only one.

And in fact, once you learn the ins and outs of these tests, the way they are desgined, and the specific techniques that make a successful test taker, the tests will seem like just another formalism in college applications. Trust me, I've been there. I aim to help you master the techniques of taking these tests which are much more important than the content itself. Knowing when to skip questions, guess on ones, focus on others, these are all things I will help you with. Of course, I will also share teach you the content of the tests and the best ways of learning for them.

Programming and Computer Science

Programming is one of the most useful abilities a beginning college student can learn. Not only will it let you stand out in college and later job applications, but it will also make you think in a completely different way than ordinary human thought. And once you are able to think more algorithmically and programmatically, the effects ripple towards all areas of your life, providing you with a skillset to face challenges that come your way. I can teach you how to program in:

  • Python:
    Without a doubt the most common and also useful language for someone looking to pursue a career in data science, scientific research, web programming, and software development (e.g. UI/UX design).

  • C:
    A more robust programming language that teaches you about the inner workings of a computer, providing a deeper understanding of computation, memory management, data structures, and algorithms. It can be a harder language to learn at first but it proves very useful not only in learning about computers but also has applications in the world of engineering, specifically electrical and mechanical engineering, where C is very commonly used.

  • JavaScript:
    An essential programming language for the develpoment of interactive, responsive, and dynamic websites and web applications. Along with a backend framework (such as flask and django) which rely on Python, and the frontend framework provided by JavaScript, your web apps will be as smooth and elegant as ever! JavaScript is also a modern language with similar abilities to Python and similar syntax to C.

Once you have learned one of these languages, you will have the necessary toolset and abilities to carry on learning other languages and diversifying your CS (computer science) and programming experience.
